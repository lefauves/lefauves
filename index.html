The Artist

John Paul Fauves is a contemporary Costa Rican painter born in 1980, based in San José. His journey began under the mentorship of Joaquín Rodríguez del Paso — one of Central America's most important modernist tutors — where he spent 15 years mastering technique before stepping into the international stage.

Fauves works in the space between Modernism and Pop Art — mixing fragments of iconic imagery into vivid, disorienting compositions. Masks, distorted pop culture symbols, layered collage: all in service of one question — who are we beneath the face we show?

His work has been exhibited at Krause Gallery (NYC), Guy Hepner (NYC), Imitate Modern (London), Meir Gallery (Belgium), ARCO Madrid, Art Basel, and Art Palm Beach. Featured in Vogue Italia and Dansk.
